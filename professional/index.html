I am currently the 10th grade Geometry teacher at Mastery Charter Schools' Shoemaker campus in Philadelphia, PA. I have previously worked in computer and data science research, on topics including pediatric medical research in the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia Research Institute's Data Science and Biostatistics Unit, and human computer interaction and natural language processing as an undergraduate at Cornell University, under the advisement Cristian Danescu-Niculescu-Mizil, Karen Levy, and Yoav Artzi. I was additionaly mentored by then-PhD-students Jonathan P. Chang and Alane Suhr. I am passionate about rigorous high school STEM education. I am also interested in research about the impacts of technology on social and economic life, and human centered computing.
